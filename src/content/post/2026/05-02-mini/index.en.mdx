## Electrical System Repairs

The air conditioner has been acting up since last year—just blowing hot air—so I'd been having it diagnosed for the past six months.  
Fortunately, it turned out not to be a compressor issue, so I went ahead with the repair during this inspection.  
The shop took their time to pinpoint the cause, and it turned out to be a faulty relay. They did some technical work replacing an internal diode.

<Image {...IMAGE_SIZES.FIXED.MDX_LG} src={Img4} alt="Electrical system work record" />

The heater's low fan speed also stopped working, so they replaced the resistor as they usually do.  
I'd like to eventually be able to handle electrical repairs myself.

## Parts That Show Wear After 100,000 km

Tie rod slackness—apparently a common issue with Minis.  
The subframe mounts also loosened up, which is pretty much what you'd expect after 100,000 km.  
I'm worried this is just the beginning and more issues will keep popping up.  
And honestly, as an amateur, I can't really tell if the steering feel changed dramatically, which is frustrating.

## Exterior Refresh

I had them replace the door handle three-piece set that I brought in.  
I considered doing it myself, but after reading online, it seemed a bit tricky, so I decided to leave it to the pros.  
The shine of the new chrome is just unbeatable.

<Image {...IMAGE_SIZES.FIXED.MDX_LG} src={Img3} alt="After door handle replacement" />

The wiper arm also ended up being replaced, and I'm very happy with the results.
