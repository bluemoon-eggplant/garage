# Yamaha MAXAM

## Overview

* Ownership period: 2007~2009
* Model year: 2007
* Grade/Color: Violet
* Displacement: 249cc
* Engine: 4-stroke water-cooled DOHC 4-valve single-cylinder
* Curb weight: 198kg
* Transmission: V-belt CVT
* Max power: 20ps
* Catalog fuel economy: 36km/L
* Fuel: Regular

## Why I Bought It

I chose a big scooter so I could ride two-up with girls.
However, it was the height of the big scooter boom at the time, and the trend was all about customization.
I had no interest in that scene, so the Majesty, Forza, and the like were off my list.
After some searching, I found the MAXAM -- wildly unique, undeniably cool, and it looked perfect for carrying a passenger!

## How I Bought It / Delivery Day

At the time, the MAXAM had just hit the market so there were no used ones available, and I bought it new.
I ordered it online, so it arrived on a flatbed truck.
Big! Cool! My very first vehicle!

## Review

Since I was a college student at the time, I almost never used the highway.
I rode this bike around Japan on a solo tour, and took it to mountains, beaches, and everywhere in between.
The standout feature was probably how little the center of gravity shifted with a passenger on back.
I recall YAMAHA even marketed this as a selling point.
Looking back, the performance was nothing special -- the footpegs would scrape if you leaned it over -- but since it was the only thing I knew, it was hands-down the most fun motorcycle experience of my life.

## Modifications

I said earlier that I wasn't into the customization trend, but apparently I did a fair bit.
Rather than the typical big-scooter look from the magazines though, mine were more on the eccentric side.
At the time, I was inspired by Wise Gear's "Royal MAXAM" complete custom package and wanted to get close to that look.
Think of it as: big-scooter version of a Gold Wing -- going for that solid, heavy presence with maximum cargo capacity and long-distance comfort.

* Front mask: One of those devil-mask style fairings
* Front fender: Chrome fender
* Step board: Chrome step board with a flare pattern
* Exhaust: WirusWin dual-exit -> SP Tadao dual-exit -> Yoshimura
* Passenger grip: Sissy bar with backrest + American-style saddlebags + American-style sissy bar rear bag
* Other: Cigarette lighter socket (wired directly to the battery!)

## Troubles

I knew nothing about mechanics back then, so I left everything to the shop.
Since I'd bought it new, that was fine, but there were still a few issues.
In particular, I hadn't considered at all the stress that my heavy luggage during the Japan tour was putting on the rear tire. Looking back, it was reckless -- it could have easily caused a serious accident.

* Headlight bulb burned out (during the Japan tour)
* Couldn't make a downhill corner and hit a rock wall (during the Japan tour)
* Rear tire puncture (right after returning home from the Japan tour)
* Crash (on an icy road)
* Tip-overs at a standstill (several times)

## Farewell

When I graduated from college and moved to Tokyo, I planned to bring the bike along.
However, to scrape together money for car accident repairs, I sold it on Yahoo Auctions.
I restored it to stock condition, sold the aftermarket parts separately, and got a total of around 300,000 yen if I recall correctly.
I rode it all the way to Tokyo and handed it over to the new owner in person at the Nerima ward insurance office.
