## Why I Bought It

DIY maintenance started with the Renaissa, and after getting my hands dirty with various jobs, I wanted to try a full car restoration next.  

* Simple mechanical design
* Good serviceability
* Cheap enough that I wouldn't cry if I broke something
* Manual transmission

Searching with these criteria, the Roadster came up as a candidate.  
Really, considering parts availability and body prices, it was the only option.  
The question was which generation to go for.  
The cheapest was the NB Roadster, but I've never liked the front-end design.  
If I'm going to lovingly maintain something, it has to be a car I actually like, so I decided to aim for the slightly pricier NA despite the premium.  
Ideally, I wanted a V-Special in green with tan interior and tan soft top, but being a popular trim, it was way out of my price range.  

## How I Bought It

For DIY maintenance, I prioritized a completely stock car above all else.  
Having aftermarket parts that prevent things from going by the service manual is a fatal problem for a beginner.  
There are cheap modified cars out there, but finding a cheap fully stock one was virtually impossible.  
However, after watching Yahoo Auctions for a while, a fully stock unit appeared. With roughly 300,000km on the odometer!  
For me, this was actually ideal -- high mileage means it was cheap, frequently driven cars tend to have healthy mechanicals, and the fact that it survived 300,000km suggests it was well-maintained.  
I contacted the seller and went to inspect it in person.  
Being in the same prefecture was a lucky break -- I could safely drive it home and save on shipping costs.  
The car's interior and exterior were remarkably clean, which was reassuring.  
Cars and motorcycles show how they've been treated on their surfaces, and a rough exterior usually means the internals are suspect too.  
This one looked way too good for a 300,000km car -- minor scratches and fading were negligible.  
But of course, a nice specimen like this meant fierce bidding on the auction, and I ended up going over budget to win it...  
Still, I figured it was better to overpay for a good car than to get burned on a problematic one for my first DIY project.  

## Delivery Day

It was my first time buying a car on Yahoo Auctions, but after coordinating with the seller, the handover went smoothly.  
I took the highway home and the car ran without a single issue. Every function worked perfectly, so I was spared from the dark side of private sales.  
Later, I went to the Land Transport Office and got to break a license plate seal for the first time in my life.

## Review

The car still had about 8 months of inspection remaining, so I put about 7,000km on it.  
Not a single critical issue. Whether it's because this particular car is a gem or because Japanese cars are just that good, I honestly questioned whether it even needed a restoration.  

### Exterior

My favorite look is with the pop-up headlights raised.
Foreign tourists have even taken photos of it.  
Also, the chrome door handles are unique and stylish.  
I feel they're a surprisingly important design element of the Roadster's overall look.  
On the functional side, the foldable side mirrors are abnormally stiff, and they feel like they might snap off at the base, so you have to be careful.  
The tail light design is also great -- a shape you don't see on modern cars, and even rare among older ones.  
It's a shame you can't see them from inside the car.  
The car came with OEM optional mud guards on the rear, but they look out of place on the rounded body. I don't think mud guards suit such a slim, streamlined car.  
The soft top is surprisingly heavy -- if you try to open and close it like an ND Roadster's, you'll hurt your arms.  
The rear acrylic window zipper is also a pain, and fitting the OEM top cover is a struggle. You just have to accept it as a classic car ritual.  

### Interior  

The steering wheel has "EUNOS" embossed on it, which gives you that feeling of "oh, this isn't a Mazda" -- a nice sense of exclusivity.
The aluminum scuff plates are also an impressive touch. (I liked them so much that I bought the re-issued new ones.)
Also, as was apparently common for cars of that era, the meter and center panel illumination is green.
At night, this glow lets you soak in some real nostalgia.  
  
### Driving Performance  

When I researched online beforehand, about 80% of the reviews seemed to just say "it's fun."
After driving it, I totally get it.
The key is the transmission.
The shift action is incredibly smooth, and the engagement is fantastic.  
I later learned it's a close-ratio gearbox.  
Highway cruising comfort suffers, but the exhilaration more than makes up for it.  
It reminded me that the ND Roadster had a similar feel, and I was impressed by the consistency of the Roadster concept across generations.  
The engine has characteristics that are very practical for daily use.  
People say it lacks low-end torque, but I think it's plenty.  
On the other hand, it doesn't really deliver torque at high RPMs, so the thrill of revving it out isn't quite there.  
In this regard, I felt the Caterham 7's engine characteristics suited me better (torque that follows through to high RPMs).  
Also, I can't tell if the OEM suspension is just worn out, but it's very soft.  
There's a lot of body roll, and it doesn't inspire you to attack corners. (It's actually scary.)  
The body rigidity is also, as expected for its age, quite floppy, so rather than pushing hard, this car seems best suited for just enjoying a pleasant, spirited drive.  
  
### Issues  

Pretty much the same impressions you read about online.

* The A/C truly does not work
* The lack of cup holders is painful
* You'd think you could put stuff behind the seats, but unless it's secured, it flies forward and becomes a hazard
* The power windows are so slow it makes you nervous
* Oil filter changes are a pain (a consequence of using a non-dedicated engine?)

## Modifications

I've been focused on keeping everything as OEM as possible.

* Swapped to OEM 14-inch BBS wheels (Enkei wheels came with the purchase)
* Replaced the head unit with the same model OEM audio
* Speakers were aftermarket from purchase (surprisingly good sound)
* Purchased OEM acrylic window mat
* Purchased OEM soft top cover

## Troubles

Troubles that occurred within 8 months of delivery.

* Scratched the rear bumper from a jack-down mishap during brake pad and fluid change
* Side brake adjustment was done incorrectly, rendering it non-functional (discovered on test drive after service, fixed immediately)
* Power steering belt snapped
* Rear license plate holder bracket broke
* Slightly unstable idle
