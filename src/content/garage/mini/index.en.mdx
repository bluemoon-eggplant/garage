## Why I Bought It

At the time, I owned the RX-7, Caterham 7, and ZZR1400.  
The RX-7 was parked in a mechanical garage one station away, and the Seven and ZZR1400 were at my home parking spot.  
I had been wanting a simple daily driver I could hop in and go, but with no place to put it, I never seriously considered it.  
One day, while casually browsing the website of the shop that serviced my Seven, I spotted a used Rover Mini for sale.  
Of course I'd known about Minis, but I'd never had any particular interest.  
But this one was light blue!  
I only knew Minis in green, so the fact that they came in that color was a surprise -- so cute! I immediately checked the price.  
It was an old car, so with initial maintenance costs factored in, the out-the-door price started at around 3 million yen.  
That was a bit much for me, so I dejectedly browsed CarSensor and found one in the same Surf Blue, with the interior fully reupholstered in light blue as well -- a truly refreshing-looking car.  
This is it! I thought, and quickly reached out and headed to see it in person.  
By the way, factory Surf Blue Minis were a limited model called Heritage Collection. In stock form, even the floor carpet is light blue, but every surviving example has worn-out seats and grubby floor carpets, so I had no desire to buy a fully stock one.  
That's why the car I found was ideal for someone who wanted a clean Surf Blue:

* Full repaint in Surf Blue
* Interior reupholstered in light blue vinyl leather
* Black floor carpet

Also, since the Caterham 7 is tiny, I realized I could barely squeeze two cars into my home parking spot, which made the purchase actually feasible.  

## How I Bought It

When I checked the actual car, it was clean at first glance but showed some wear in the details -- fair for the price.
The minimal rust on the body and undercarriage was also a good sign.  
The thing I valued most was that it had been converted from AT to MT.  
With over 100,000km on the clock, oil leaks were a concern, but if the transmission had been swapped, the gaskets and such should have been replaced during that process.  
Above all, the fact that someone loved it enough to swap the transmission was the deciding factor.

## Review

It's a car with plenty of problems, but its toughness as a reliable daily driver (for its age) deserves credit.  
In the vintage compact car category, the Beetle and FIAT 500 often come up, but the Mini should be overwhelmingly more reliable than those.

### Exterior

It's no exaggeration to say the entire reason to own this car is its looks -- it's adorable.  
Because the body is so small and simple, the wheels inevitably become a dominant visual element, so you absolutely need to keep clean ones on it.  
I had my eye on SSR Mesh wheels since I like mesh-style wheels, but only beat-up used ones were available, so I gave up on that.  
Personally, the front grille and stainless steel bumpers are what make me feel "this is a Mini" the most.  
Just looking at them makes me happy.

### Interior

The wood panel and white gauge combination is superb -- it makes you feel special while driving.  
The car came with a NARDI wood steering wheel, which might be a touch flashy? But I prefer the simpler NARDI over a Moto-Lita, so it's fine.  
Having the entire interior unified in light blue looks incredibly cute from outside, and even a stock-purist like me thinks "yeah, this works."

### Driving Performance

Frankly, I don't think this is a car you discuss in terms of driving performance.  
The media calls it a "go-kart feeling," but I honestly don't get it.  
It's just slow and powerless.  
I always get left behind at traffic lights.  
With only 4 gears, cruising at 100km/h on the highway is pretty noisy.  
On top of that, the center of gravity seems surprisingly high? Front-heavy? So cornering isn't fun either.  
Downhill corners in particular are scary -- if you carry too much speed, you get the feeling the outside front tire is about to give up.  
With nothing to really shine about, I've accepted it as a utilitarian daily driver and left it at that.

### Issues

No major breakdowns yet, but something needs fixing at every inspection.  
I can accept rubber parts wearing out, but when things like the front brake back plate crack, you really feel the fragility of British car metal components.  
Also, because they crammed as much mechanical stuff as possible into such a small body, serviceability is poor.  
Oil consumption mysteriously increases in winter, switches break causing lights to stop working, so routine inspections are critically important for this car.

## Modifications

The car was already modified when I bought it, so I haven't made major changes.  
To be honest, I don't even know exactly what the stock spec was, so some of this is guesswork.

* Full repaint (Surf Blue-ish, but lighter than factory Surf Blue -- more of a sky blue)
* Interior color change (light blue)
* Aftermarket dash panel
* NARDI wood steering wheel
* Hi-Lo kit installed
* Coil spring conversion
* Door and window handles replaced with wood parts
* Wood shift knob installed
* Minilite reproduction wheels (for summer tires)
* OEM steel wheels painted + stainless wheel caps (for winter tires)
* OEM door visors installed
* Converted to fender mirrors

## Troubles

* One-side brake light not lighting (socket contact issue)
* Brake lights not lighting (brake switch contact issue)
* Rain leak
