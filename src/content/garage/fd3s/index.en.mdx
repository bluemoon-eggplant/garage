## Why I Bought It

I first learned about this car during college, thanks to Initial D.  
It wasn't like I was dead-set on the RX-7 from the start -- I was also looking at the Silvia (S15), S2000, and Supra (JZA80). In the end, the unique character of the rotary engine and the styling won me over.  
The Supra had high taxes, the S2000 was expensive even used, and I could never warm up to the Silvia's rear styling, so they all dropped off the list.  

## How I Bought It

Since I could only afford something cheap, I searched CarSensor without caring about accident history or unpopular colors.  
I did want to avoid high-mileage cars though.  
At the time, I wanted one in Innocent Blue Mica but couldn't find one, so when I spotted a unit with 18,000km, accident history, a Type 6, for 1.18 million yen, I reached out.  
I couldn't inspect the actual car since it was at a different location, but they let me sit in a different RX-7, and it was shocking -- can a human actually fit in this thing? (Way too cramped.)  
I figured a novice like me wouldn't learn much from inspecting it in person anyway, so I trusted the dealer and bought it. Turned out to be a good call -- the dealer was a decent person.

## Delivery Day

This turned out to be the worst delivery day in my history of car ownership.
First, I went to pick up the car despite a snow forecast.  
I was too excited to care.  
But at the very first intersection after delivery, the rear slid out, and then I got stuck on a hill.  
People waiting at a bus stop had to push my car so I could turn around.  
I'm pretty sure my face was redder than the car's paint from embarrassment.  
I took an alternate route and somehow made it to a supermarket near my house.  
I was directed to the second-floor parking lot, but couldn't make it up due to the slippery surface. Going back down in reverse, the tires locked up whenever I hit the brakes. That was genuinely terrifying.  
I ended up asking the supermarket if I could leave my car in their lot overnight and barely made it home alive.  

## Review

### Exterior

The fact that I still own this car says it all -- its appeal hasn't faded one bit.  
There aren't many low-slung cars like this anymore, and it only looks more alluring with each passing year.  
That said, as I've gotten older, I think the subtler wing of the early/mid-model years suits it better than the flashy wing on the Type 5/6.  
I mostly agree with the opinion that the Italian design concept doesn't mesh well with the later model's flashiness.  
I've also grown to really love the red body color.

### Interior

Personally, the white gauges with red illumination are the best.  
The car came with a factory audio system with a 6-disc CD changer, and I'm fond of the sound quality.  
It's famously known for having no cup holders. I used to attach one to the A/C vent, but after committing to preserving the OEM look, I removed everything.  
Now I have a DIY cup holder installed behind the center console.  

### Driving Performance

For an amateur like me, it's simply a fast car.  
Right after delivery, knowing nothing, I floored it in 2nd gear and remember being genuinely scared.  
However, after an accident, the trauma has kept me from properly getting on the throttle. (Even now.)  
Also, the cornering on this car is insane.  
It can take corners at incredible speeds with total stability, but as a novice driver, I can't sense where the limit is. And that's what's scary.  
To give an extreme example, the Rover Mini is so front-heavy that in downhill corners you can feel when the front tires are about to wash out (understeer), but with the RX-7, you get absolutely no such feedback.  
That said, I do love the rotary engine feel.  
It barely moves at low RPM, but the way it smoothly winds up as the revs climb is a sensation you can't get from any other car.  
As someone who uses this car as a GT cruiser, I also appreciate the rotary's high refinement and low vibration.

### Issues

The biggest issue is the low body rigidity -- a well-documented topic.  
It's especially noticeable on rough rural roads, where vibrations from bumps take a while to settle.  
Compared to the S2000, the RX-7's floor is noticeably more flexible, and you can feel the body twisting.  
Since I'm an amateur, the effect on speed doesn't bother me, but the negative impact on ride comfort is something I definitely notice.

## Modifications

With an accident loan to pay off, I couldn't afford modifications, and before I knew it, I'd become a stock-purist.  
I also got spooked by forum posts saying that even just changing the exhaust can shorten a rotary's lifespan.

* Laid down a trunk rubber mat for dirt protection
* Replaced the floor carpet with the optional red carpet
* Swapped the front strut tower bar to the OEM red type
* Replaced the suspension with KYB OEM-equivalent units since the originals had worn out

## Troubles

I could write endlessly, but there haven't been any fatal issues like an engine blow.  
At this point, there might be almost no factory-original paint left due to all the bodywork repairs.

* Accident one month after delivery (front-end major damage)
* Dead battery deep in the mountains
* Hit a wall on a super narrow road (left door major damage)
* Clipped the left side step while turning left in Sangenjaya
* Engine wouldn't rev while driving on the Shuto Expressway (solenoid valve failure)
* Window wouldn't close during a DIY vehicle inspection (window switch failure)
* Fender bender at Tatsumi PA (moderate damage around the right door)
* Hit one of those mystery width-restriction poles common in Saitama (left front fender moderate damage)
* My own motorcycle tipped over onto the car (right front fender moderate damage)
* Coolant warning wouldn't stop deep in the mountains, had to be towed (radiator cap deterioration)
* Engine wouldn't start at a supermarket parking lot, had to be towed (clutch switch failure)
* Dead battery in a mechanical parking garage (left the interior light on)
