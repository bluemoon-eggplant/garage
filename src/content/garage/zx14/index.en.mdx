## Why I Bought It

Back in college.
I was flipping through a motorcycle magazine at a friend's place when I came across a bike that blew my mind.
That was the ZZR1400.
First of all, the 6-eye headlight design was wildly unusual.
More than the specs, it was that striking design that drew me in.
I didn't have my large-displacement license at the time, so it was just a vague dream of "someday I'll ride this bike."
Fast forward to 2021 -- even 10 years after that first encounter, the desire hadn't faded.
I started searching for bikes while attending driving school.

## How I Bought It

Initially, the color I was looking for was the Metallic Ocean Blue, the one I'd first seen in the magazine.
But as I browsed around, the orange-and-black two-tone caught my eye.
I loved how only the side cowl fins were color-split, and the fact that it was a Special Edition sealed the deal.
I searched Goo Bike for a fully stock, low-mileage unit and went to inspect it.
With only about 12,000km on it, the price tag read 1.07 million yen -- on the pricey side.
On top of that, the left under cowl had a crack.
So the value wasn't great, but given how few Special Edition orange units exist, I compromised and bought it.

## Delivery Day

Unlike my college days, being an adult means proper risk management.
I bought the full set in advance -- full-face helmet, gloves, body armor, and boots.
Loaded down with gear in the middle of summer, I made my way to the bike shop by train, bus, and on foot.
It had been 10 years since I'd last ridden a motorcycle, and this was a seriously heavy bike, so I was nervous for the first ride. But once I got going, it was so easy and stable that it felt more like driving a car than riding a bike. I made it home safely.
I'd have no hesitation jumping straight on the highway with this thing -- it's incredibly user-friendly.

## Review

On my 250cc bike, I was always frustrated by how much wind and road conditions affected the ride.
By contrast, the ZX-14 proves that weight equals stability -- the difference is night and day.

### Exterior

The sheer presence gives you a tremendous sense of ownership.
The imposing front face is great, of course, but the fat dual exhaust at the rear is stunning.
The successor ZX-14R comes with blacked-out OEM exhaust, but personally I prefer the metallic look.
The cowl's wind protection is excellent, making it a proper highway touring machine -- one of my favorite aspects.
Also, being a white-gauge purist, the instrument cluster design is perfect!

### Riding Position & Handling

How aggressive the forward lean feels is subjective, but for me it was a non-issue.
However, since it was my first time with clip-on handlebars, I remember being surprised at how little steering lock there was.
Due to the wide body, you can't get both feet flat on the ground.
I'm grateful my parents gave me the height to ride this thing.
As for handling at low speeds, it's the worst.
You especially need to watch the slope of your parking spot -- get it wrong and you literally can't get the bike out by yourself.
The moment you lose your balance, the weight makes recovery impossible, so tip-overs at a standstill are a frequent occurrence.

### Riding Performance

The speed and acceleration are on an insane level -- misjudge the throttle and you'll feel like you're about to be launched off the bike.
It's faster than anything else I own, and that alone makes this bike worth having.
However, especially after buying the Renaissa, I've become acutely aware that its weight makes you reluctant to push through corners.
Even so, I consider it a bike defined by its straight-line acceleration above all else.

## Modifications

It came fenderless from the start, which I like because it accentuates the rear-end styling.
Along with the delivery service, I had a front/rear dashcam, ETC, USB power, frame sliders, and front/rear axle sliders installed.
After that, I just added the OEM single-seat cowl.
There's really nothing you'd call a proper modification.

## Troubles

All the trouble comes from its weight.

* Lost balance while looking directly behind me at a stop -- tipped over
* Tipped over in my home parking spot and crashed directly into the RX-7, leaving a big dent
* Thought the kickstand was down but it wasn't -- tipped over
* Tipped over while backing out of my home parking spot
