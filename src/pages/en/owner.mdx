I'm just an ordinary guy who loves cars and motorcycles.
Let me introduce myself.

# Vehicles and Me

## I Love Traveling

For me, cars and motorcycles are a means of travel, so I hardly ever join group touring events.
I also don't have the desire to show off my cars, so I'm not interested in car exhibition events.
That said, I don't mind being approached by people on the road, and I try to respond as kindly as possible.

## Tools, First and Foremost

Because I love traveling, I inevitably cover long distances, and I value reliability as a practical tool.
I also believe tools are meant to be used, so I don't worry much about stone chips and minor scratches.
My motto has been "ride hard, fix it, repeat," but before I knew it, all my vehicles have become elderly machines, and I feel a bit of self-contradiction.

---

# How I Met Vehicles

## Meeting Motorcycles

I had no connection to vehicles until a turning point in my second year of college.
A close friend bought a motorcycle (HONDA GB250 Clubman) and gave me a ride on the back.
The overwhelming speed, acceleration — everything was uncharted territory. (Along with the absurd vibrations unique to the Clubman.)
There's a Japanese expression "having your brain burned" — and that's exactly what happened. My brain was burned by the engine.
For the first time, I wanted a motorcycle of my own.

## Meeting Cars

This is a very common story, but my interest in cars came from watching Initial D during college.
Before that, I had virtually zero interest.
In my senior year, with the optimistic assumption that I could pay off a loan once I started working, I bought my first car.

---

# Preferences

First and foremost, I'm a purist.
I believe the factory condition designed by the manufacturer is the most beautiful, so I prefer to keep things stock.
No matter how dissatisfied I am with a particular aspect, I accept it as the philosophy and background behind its creation, and interpret it as "character."
That said, since buying the Mini, my rigid views have softened a bit.

## Design

### Cars

In my personal theory, car design can be divided into two categories:

* American style
* European style

I prefer the "European style" and find beauty in curvy forms.
My origin point is the "RX-7 FD3S" — that was my gateway to the designs I love.
With full awareness that this is an extreme position, I assert that "long nose, short deck" should be prioritized above all else.

### Motorcycles

My love for "curvy" design is the same as with cars.
The first motorcycle that struck me was the "KAWASAKI ZZR1400," and I generally love YAMAHA's design language.

## Performance

### Cars

In contrast to my design preferences, I don't have strong opinions about performance.
What's certain is that I value lightness over power.
If I had to specify an engine preference, I favor power delivery that builds in high RPMs.
I believe the limit for enjoying this on public roads is around a naturally aspirated 4-cylinder 1.6L, and the Caterham 7's engine characteristics come closest to my ideal.
The NA Roadster has the same package, but it produces hardly any power at high revs, so I consider it a fundamentally different experience.

### Motorcycles

On the other hand, with motorcycles, I'm an inline-four sound supremacist.
However, since owning the V-twin Renaissa, that conviction has mellowed.
Meanwhile, the balance between weight and power is a difficult topic — I can't answer whether I prefer large or small bikes.
Light bikes make cornering fun, but they simply lack stability and feel dangerous; heavy bikes are the opposite — each has its pros and cons.
That's precisely why I feel motorcycles can be more fun than cars in certain ways.
